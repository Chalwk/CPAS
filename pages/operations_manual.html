CPAS Operations Manual

Basic guidelines for virtual charter operations

General Operating Rules

1. Virtual, Not Real

Remember this is simulation. Follow real-world principles, but don't stress over perfection.

2. Have Fun

The primary goal is enjoyable, realistic virtual flying. Don't make it a chore.

3. Respect Limits

Fly within your skill level and aircraft capabilities. No need to prove anything.

4. Help Others

We're a community. Help new pilots, share tips, be supportive.

Pre-Flight Procedures

Basic Pre-Flight (All Flights)

  1. Choose Aircraft: Select appropriate aircraft for route and rank
  2. Check Weather: Use in-game weather or add-ons. Don't fly in impossible conditions
  3. Flight Plan: Know your route, alternates, and fuel requirements
  4. Checklists: Complete the appropriate checklist for your aircraft
  5. Briefing: For complex flights, consider a quick self-briefing

Charter-Specific Preparation

  • Passenger Count: Don't exceed aircraft capacity
  • Route Knowledge: Familiarize with terrain, airspace, challenges
  • Alternates: Always have a backup plan (weather, mechanical)
  • Time Planning: Allow buffer for delays

In-Flight Operations

Communication
  • Use appropriate frequencies (see routes)
  • No ATC? Use Common Traffic Advisory Frequency (CTAF) concepts
  • Position reports on longer flights (self-announce)
Terrain & Weather
  • Maintain safe altitude above terrain
  • Turn back if weather deteriorates
  • Mountain flying: know valley directions and escape routes
Fuel Management
  • Land with at least 45 minutes fuel reserve
  • Monitor fuel consumption in mountains (higher burn)
  • Plan refueling stops on long routes
Passenger Comfort
  • Smooth takeoffs and landings
  • Gentle turns and altitude changes
  • Point out scenery (in your imagination!)
