CPAS Operations Manual

Basic guidelines for virtual charter operations

General Operating Rules

1. Virtual, Not Real

Remember this is simulation. Follow real-world principles, but don't stress over perfection.

2. Have Fun

The primary goal is enjoyable, realistic virtual flying. Don't make it a chore.

3. Respect Limits

Fly within your skill level and aircraft capabilities. No need to prove anything.

4. Help Others

We're a community. Help new pilots, share tips, be supportive.

5. Airline Identification

Use ICAO airline code CPX for all flight planning and reporting purposes.

Discord Bot Commands

Flight reporting and management through our Discord bot

/pirep

Standard Flights

Submit a PIREP using your latest SimBrief flight plan for normal, scheduled flights.

Usage:

/pirep [Pilot ID] [status (optional)]

Parameters:
  • pilot id: Your SimBrief numeric Pilot/User ID (required)
  • status: Flight status (completed, in-progress, scheduled, cancelled, diverted)
Notes:
  • Automatically fetches your latest SimBrief flight plan
  • For standard passenger/cargo charter flights
  • Posts to #pirep-submission channel

/missionreport

Special Operations

Submit special mission reports for non-standard operations not supported by SimBrief.

Usage:

/missionreport [type] [aircraft] [callsign] ...

Mission Types:
  • Search & Rescue (SAR)
  • Medical Evacuation (MEDEVAC)
  • Patient Transfer
  • Firefighting Support
  • Aerial Tours
  • Law Enforcement
  • VIP Transport
  • Training Flights
Notes:
  • For HEMS, SAR, tours, training, and other non-scheduled ops
  • Custom flight reporting without SimBrief requirement
  • Supports detailed mission parameters

/updateflight

Flight Management

Update the status of an existing flight record in our database.

Usage:

/updateflight [flight ID] [new status]

Parameters:
  • flight id: Flight ID (e.g., CPX1234)
  • status: New status (completed, in-progress, scheduled, cancelled, diverted)
Notes:
  • Update flight status as your operation progresses
  • Only update your own flights (admins can update any)
  • Automatic notifications in #pirep-submission channel

Pre-Flight Procedures

Basic Pre-Flight (All Flights)

  1. Choose Aircraft: Select an appropriate aircraft for the route and your rank
  2. Check Weather: Live Weather is required for all CPAS flights. Review current conditions and do not fly in impossible or unsafe weather
  3. Flight Plan: Know your route, alternates, and fuel requirements
  4. Checklists: Complete the appropriate checklist for your aircraft
  5. Briefing: For complex flights, conduct a brief self-briefing

Charter-Specific Preparation

  • Route Knowledge: Familiarize with terrain, airspace, challenges
  • Alternates: Always have a backup plan (weather, mechanical)
  • Time Planning: Allow buffer for delays

In-Flight Operations

Communication
  • Use appropriate frequencies (see routes)
  • No ATC? Use Common Traffic Advisory Frequency (CTAF) concepts
  • Position reports on longer flights (self-announce)
Terrain & Weather
  • Maintain safe altitude above terrain
  • Turn back if weather deteriorates
  • Mountain flying: know valley directions and escape routes
Fuel Management
  • Land with at least 45 minutes fuel reserve
  • Monitor fuel consumption in mountains (higher burn)
  • Plan refueling stops on long routes
Passenger Comfort
  • Smooth takeoffs and landings
  • Gentle turns and altitude changes
  • Point out scenery (in your imagination!)
