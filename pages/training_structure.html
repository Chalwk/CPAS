CPAS Training Structure

A progressive training system designed for virtual charter operations

Rank Structure

Our ranks reflect a charter operation rather than airline hierarchy:

1

Pilot Under Training

Requirements: Newly accepted pilots

Privileges:

  • Access to training aircraft (typically Piper Warrior)
  • Must fly with instructor oversight or approved alternative aircraft
  • Local area familiarization flights

Aircraft Flexibility: While the Piper Warrior is our primary trainer, pilots uncomfortable with this aircraft may request alternative training arrangements with an instructor.

Upgrade to: Complete 5 training flights & basic navigation test

2

Charter Pilot

Requirements: Basic training complete

Privileges:

  • Can fly simple charter routes solo
  • Access to basic fleet (Warrior, R44)
  • Day VFR operations only
  • Can transport passengers on approved routes

Upgrade to: 10 completed charters & mountain flying basics

3

Senior Charter Pilot

Requirements: Proven charter experience

Privileges:

  • Access to entire fleet (except H145)
  • Can fly complex/mountain routes
  • May mentor Pilot Under Training
  • Can request custom charters

Upgrade to: 25 completed charters & turbine endorsement

4

Lead Pilot

Requirements: Extensive charter & mountain experience

Privileges:

  • Full fleet access including H145
  • Can approve new pilots for solo
  • May develop new routes
  • Special operations (glacier landings, etc.)

Note: This is our highest operational rank

I

Instructor

Requirements: Exceptional knowledge & teaching ability

Responsibilities:

  • Conduct check rides
  • Train new pilots
  • Maintain training standards
  • Approve rank advancements

Note: Separate from operational chain, focused on training

Training Programs

Fixed-Wing Training

1
Basic Handling

Piper Tomahawk/Warrior - Circuits, emergencies, basic navigation

2
Cross-Country

Piper Archer - Flight planning, airspace, passenger procedures

3
Complex Operations

Piper Arrow - Retractable gear, performance, mountain flying

Helicopter Training

1
Basic Rotorcraft

R44 - Hovering, confined areas, basic autorotations

2
Turbine Transition

R66 - Engine management, increased performance

3
Mountain & Utility

H125/H145 - High altitude, sling loads, glacier operations

Basic Training Rules

1. Training Flights

All new pilots complete 3 training flights before solo charter

2. Route Progression

Start with simple routes, progress to complex as rank increases

3. Checklists Required

Use aircraft checklists for every flight - they exist for a reason

4. Ask Questions

Unsure about something? Ask in Discord before flying

Important Notes

  • This is a virtual operation - focus on learning and having fun!
  • Progress at your own pace - no pressure
  • All training follows real-world aviation principles adapted for simulation
  • Instructors are volunteers passionate about aviation
  • The goal is realistic enjoyment and skill-building, not strict certification. That said, if you want to challenge yourself with a more comprehensive Pilot Training Program, we encourage users to participate in the Pilot Training Program on VATSIM. Further reading is available in the VATSIM Learning Center.
